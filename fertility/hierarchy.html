Hierarchy

In life, there is a hierarchy to two joining families, in the way of crossbreeding. There is a "Queen Female", biologically pre-existing, in one species, while in the other, there is a "King Male". In order for their species to continue in growth together, being the first in line for procreation, organically, they must commit to one another and have children together at some stage, while the King Male lives.

If the King Male dies or becomes ill, past a certain amount, another male is in line within the Queen Female's family, or species, for the continuation of life. The first King Male is only fertile towards the Queen Female. When the first King Male dies, if it is before procreating offspring, that level of depth is lost in life and crossbreeding has failed.

A "Singular Mate" is the initial mate for a person. The Singular Mate is an instance of one of a binary pair, between two, that is either male or female. The female's soul pre-exists the male's, whose soul is made out of the female's. The male's body is born in life, of the Singular Mates, before the female's body is born, where they are both living.

When we are considering a King or Queen within a species, the two, first, merging to crossbreed between two species; if the King Male has died or been overcome with illness, of the two, fertility transitions to the second male, the twin of the Queen Female, within their species. The species, or family, goes down a level in complexity, into inbreeding.

The species or the family of the King Male dies, and life goes into mourning. The species from whence the King Male died, goes into inbreeding with a set of Singular Mates younger than the King Male.

If there are further losses, such as the twin of the Queen Female, fertility transitions to their younger brother, with her, remaining in inbreeding. All three males are the Singular Mate of the Queen Female. All three are made to take care of the Queen Female as her husbands.

If the Queen Female dies, then that level of depth of complexity is lost within the species. It would not be until another birth of a Queen Female and King Male, that there would be another chance of crossbreeding between species.
